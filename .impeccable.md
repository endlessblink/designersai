## Design Context

### Users
Designers with AI serves artists, designers, curators, cultural partners, press, and prospective community members who need to understand the network, see its creative credibility, and find a path to participate or collaborate.

### Brand Personality
Editorial, cultural, refined. The site should feel like an international exhibition platform and living artist community, not a SaaS product or generic AI startup.

### Aesthetic Direction
The current direction is restrained editorial minimalism: warm paper neutrals, deep charcoal, serif display typography, generous whitespace, and exhibition-led media. The interface should become richer through real creator work, strong art direction, and clearer editorial hierarchy rather than decorative gradients or glowing AI visuals.

### Design Principles
- Lead with cultural proof: exhibitions, artists, practice, and real work should be visible before abstract claims.
- Treat creators as the product: community sections need portraits, artworks, bios, links, and profile paths, not name chips.
- Keep forms pragmatic: collect links and materials for approval before investing in account infrastructure.
- Preserve bilingual dignity: Hebrew pages should feel intentionally RTL, not translated after the fact.
- Avoid generic AI aesthetics: no neon glow, glassmorphism, purple-blue startup gradients, or template card grids without editorial purpose.
